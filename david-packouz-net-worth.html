Multi-talented businessman, inventor, and musician David Packouz’s net worth is a whopping $2.9 million.

Like a rocket, their company took off. They received their first order shortly after founding the company.

The delivery of 50,000 gallons of propane to an Air Force base in Wyoming was part of a US government contract.

On the first deal, they made an $8,000 profit. That may not seem like much, but it was just the start.

They had secured over 100 contracts worth over $100 million to sell ammunition and weapons to the US government in just a few months.

Moreover, they received 149 contracts for roughly $10.5 million by 2006.

Their company was awarded a roughly $300 million US government contract in early 2007 to supply the Afghan Army with 100 million AK-47 rounds, millions of SVD Dragunov sniper rifles, and aircraft rockets.

David was making money hand over fist, and there was no end to it.

The quicker someone gets to success, the faster they fall! They proved this quote with the downfall of their business.

Additionally, their company had failed to deliver on several contracts, including delivering potentially dangerous helmets to Iraq and failing to deliver 10,000 Beretta pistols.

During an examination by the governmental organization, papers revealed that federal agencies had terminated, withdrew, or suspended at least seven prior contracts with AEY due to poor quality or late delivery.

Hence, they were investigated and found guilty after receiving much media attention.

David Packouz | Career

Packouz and his business associate Efraim Diveroli decided to enter the armaments trade in 2005. The field was dominated at the time by a small, close-knit group of merchants.

It was also a company that necessitated the ability to establish high-level ties within the US administration as well as with foreign countries.

Packouz and Diveroli were unfazed by any of this. They started with only a few contacts, a little money, and no prior expertise.

Packouz, a massage therapist, was only 23 years old at the time, while Diveroli was 19 years old. Within a few years, they made millions of dollars in this high-risk, high-reward company.
