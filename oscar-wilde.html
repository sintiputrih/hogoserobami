Biography

Oscar Wilde was born in Dublin, Ireland on October 16, 1854. Irish playwright, poet, and novelist who is best known for The Importance of Being Earnest and The Picture of Dorian Gray. He is also famous for his memorable, witty epigrams. He was a contemporary and acquaintance of the French symbolist poet Stephane Mallarme. He studied at Trinity College, Dublin, and at Magdalen College, Oxford, and was known during his university years for his humorously eccentric persona. He published his collected Poems in 1881 when he was twenty-seven years old.
