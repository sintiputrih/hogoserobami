Zach and Julie Ertz: A Dynamic Duo On and Off the Field

Zach Ertz, a Super Bowl winner with the Philadelphia Eagles, and his wife Julie Ertz, a World Cup champion with the United States Women's National Soccer Team, are not only a power couple in the world of sports but also a true testament to love, dedication, and making a positive impact on and off the field. 

A Sporting Power Couple

Zach and Julie Ertz's love story began at a baseball game in 2012, where Zach, a football player, and Julie, a soccer player, met.

Their relationship blossomed despite the challenges of long-distance dating as their athletic careers soared.

Their journey has seen numerous milestones, from Julie's World Cup victory in 2015 to Zach's Super Bowl win with the Philadelphia Eagles.

Personal Milestones

In 2017, the couple tied the knot, and in 2022, they welcomed their first child, Madden Matthew, into the world. Also, in the summer of 2024, they joyfully announced that they are expecting their second child.

This progression in their personal lives has only strengthened their bond as a couple.

Balancing Careers and Marriage

Both Zach and Julie have had to navigate the demands of professional sports while nurturing their marriage.

Julie described their marriage as a "blessing," emphasizing the importance of understanding the dedication required to reach the top of their respective sports, which has helped them support each other through their careers.

Impact Beyond the Field

Zach and Julie Ertz are not only known for their achievements on the field but also for their philanthropic endeavors. In 2018, they founded the Ertz Family Foundation with a focus on creating sports and educational opportunities for youth and restoring hope for families in need. 

Making a Difference

The Ertz Family Foundation's involvement in supporting youth sports, education, and family enrichment has had a tangible impact.

In one instance, the foundation provided a $10,000 grant to the Kensington High School football team after their equipment was stolen, enabling them to salvage their season and continue playing.

Advice and Influences

Zach Ertz's career has been shaped by influential mentors and experiences. He emphasizes the importance of staying focused, surrounding oneself with supporters, and dedicating oneself to avoid regrets. Also, his former coach, Brent Jones, played a pivotal role in instilling belief in Zach's potential to become an NFL Tight End.

Inspirational Quotes and Life Lessons

Both Zach and Julie Ertz draw inspiration from meaningful quotes and life lessons. Zach cites the quote, "Don't mistake activity for achievement" by John Wooden, emphasizing the significance of making every minute count in the pursuit of goals. 

Impactful Causes and Movements

Zach and Julie's focus on education and access to youth sports as the most important movement to them highlights the value they place on providing opportunities for learning, teamwork, and a sense of belonging to the younger generation.

Media and Partnerships

The couple leverages their influence and media partnerships to raise awareness for their foundation's mission, utilizing social media and their foundation's website as platforms to reach a larger audience.

The Personal Side

Their love story is not just about sports; it's about two individuals who found their greatest supporter in each other. ESPN's feature, "E-60: Meet the Ertzes," captures moments that showcase their unwavering support for each other, such as Julie's emotional reaction upon learning of Zach's team's victory while she was playing for the US National team. 

 

The compelling journey of Zach and Julie Ertz goes beyond their success on the field. It encompasses their deep-rooted commitment to making a positive impact on the world around them, their unwavering support for each other, and the values they uphold.

Their love story is not just about the victories in their careers but about the victories they share as a couple, as they continue to inspire others through their remarkable partnership both on and off the field.
