Early Life

Kendrick Lamar, originally Kendrick Lamar Duckworth, was born on June the 17th, 1987, in Compton, California, United States of America.

His father, Kenny Duckworth, was a gangster follower.

He was grown in the rough neighborhood of Compton around gang members, with his closest friends being Westside Piru Blood.

His mother gave his name Kendrick in honor of American singer Eddie Kendrick of The Temptations.

Lamar grew up on welfare and in Section 8 housing.

He was just eight years of age in 1995 when he witnessed the filming of the music video for the hit single “California Love,” which proved to be an important moment in his life.

He idolizes Tupac Shakur and Dr. Dre.

He attended McNair Elementary and Vanguard Learning Center in Compton Unified School District as a child.

He was kind of a loner until the age of seven. After passing out at the school level, he attended Centennial High School in Compton, where he was a straight-A student.

Kendrick Lamar | Career

Lamar was still in high school and 16 years old when he released his first mixtape named Youngest Head Nigga in Charge.

In the early 2000s, he signed with Top Dawg Entertainment and released another mixtape which garnered more attention.

Later, he was signed to Lil Wayne and started calling himself Kendrick Lamar, losing his previous stage name, K. Dot.
