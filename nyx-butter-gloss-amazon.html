The 16 Best Mascaras for Short Eyelashes

In the era of dramatic lash extensions and false eyelashes that are actually easy to apply, the classic mascara might be ignored — and that would be a mistake, especially if you have short lashes. Mascara is a low-maintenance, highly effective way to quickly lengthen, volumize, and even strengthen your growth-challenged eyelashes. Some mascaras even have innovative wands and brushes that help you work with the lashes you’ve got for a dramatic, eye-catching look that’s all you.

There are literally hundreds of mascaras out there, and not all were meant for the short-lashed among us. We went straight to the pros — like celebrity makeup artists Jamie Greenberg and Nydia Figueroa and beauty expert Katya Bychkova — to understand which ingredients, formulas, and wands enhance diminutive lashes. After hearing their expertise and reflecting on our own experience as beauty editors who test mascaras on the regular, we can say with confidence these are the best mascaras for short lashes.

And, although this product grips onto lashes for all-day wear, it’s easy to wash off with warm water and soap.

What to Keep in Mind

Ingredients

Depending on the formula, mascaras can be made with a range of different ingredients. You can find nourishing additions like castor oil and shea butter in some (Tarte Tartelette Tubing Mascara features both ingredients), which help to hydrate lashes and keep the formula from flaking. Other mascaras might include argan or marula oils, which work to create volume. Additionally, if you want clean or vegan mascaras, be sure to note what ingredients are not listed, like parabens, sulfates, talc, and mineral oil. Ilia Limitless Lash Lengthening Clean Mascara has a top-notch clean formula.

Wand

The beauty of mascara wands is that they come in all different shapes and sizes. Some wands are made to lengthen lashes, many are crafted to add volume, and others are used to create a curl. Based on your preferences, you can find a mascara with a wand that specifically caters to your needs. 

For instance, if you want a mascara that is lengthening, look for a wand that has sharp, rubberized bristles, like Tower 28 MakeWaves Lengthening + Curling Mascara. These can help to separate and elongate each individual lash. If you are hoping to amp up your eye makeup with bold, voluminous mascara, consider getting a product that has a fatter wand and more bristles. (Our best overall pick Rare Beauty Perfect Strokes Universal Volumizing Mascara has a fluffy brush made for adding mega volume.) A thicker wand is able to coat multiple lashes at once, which makes it easier to build on top of previous coats. As for a mascara that will lift and curl lashes, we suggest looking for a curved wand. This will help create, set, and keep the curl in place once mascara is applied. The scooped wand on Benefit Cosmetics Roller Lash Curling & Lifting Mascara is ideal for lifting and curling.

Your Questions, Answered

What is the best way to apply mascara to short lashes?

To add length to short lashes with mascara, it is important to apply the product in a very specific way. “Start at the root [of eyelashes], push in, and wiggle upwards,” Greenberg explains. “I also do more coats on the outer corner of the lashes to make them flare open.” 

Additionally, there are supplemental products that can be used with mascara to enhance short lashes. “When it comes to short lashes, using a lash curler is a must,” Bychkova notes. “This tool helps to visually open your eyes and create an illusion of longer lashes.” A mascara primer is also helpful. “This is an excellent investment, especially if you want length and volume to your lashes. For years, my favorite has been Dior Diorshow Maximizer 3D Lash Primer; it adds definition to your lashes and makes your lashes appear longer and thicker,” she adds. Some mascaras also come with a built in primer, like our best two-in-one pick Honest Beauty Extreme Length Mascara + Lash Primer.

On top of applying mascara correctly and using eyelash curlers and primers, you can also implement different techniques into your routine. Bychkova recommends what she calls the “tight line technique.” This consists of applying eyeliner in between lashes to help create the illusion of thicker lashes.

What is the best way to remove mascara?

According to Greenberg, using an oil cleanser or makeup remover is the safest way to remove mascara. “A good cleanser should also hopefully get it off,” she adds. This is because oil-based cleansers can easily break down mascara formulas without damaging lashes in the process. 

Not surprisingly, makeup removers are also a fool-proof way to take off mascara. To get the best and safest results from a makeup remover, wet a cotton round with the solution, then gently rub eyes. “Never use the same cotton round on both eyes, though: If there is an infection in one of your eyes, you risk spreading it to both eyes,” notes Bychkova.

In terms of being eco-conscious, there are also other things to use besides cotton pads. “A quick tip: You can get a set of reusable makeup-removing pads in order to stop buying cotton rounds and save the environment,” says Bychkova. “My favorite ones are Garnier SkinActive Micellar Cleansing Eco Pads that you can get for just $10 for a set of three. After each use, wash the reusable pads with a facial cleanser and leave them drying overnight on a towel. Give a deep machine wash with a sensitive skin suitable detergent once a week.”

Aside from gentle cleansers and makeup-removing products, it is also important to be mindful of how you are taking off mascara. “The most important thing to remember when removing mascara is to avoid excessive rubbing,” explains Bychkova. “It might irritate the sensitive skin around the eye area; if you are rubbing too much, you might also pull out some lashes.”

How can you make eyelashes longer without using harmful products?

While lash extensions might seem like a quick fix to make short lashes longer, they can often harm them. To avoid this, opt for other ways to make lashes look longer. “Adding a mascara primer to your routine before applying your mascara makes a world of a difference when making your lashes appear longer,” explains Figueroa. “You can also try a lash serum that is safe for the eye area with great results. My top pick for an eyelash serum is the RapidLash Eyelash Enhancing Serum — It’s jam-packed with vitamins to fortify your lashes and make them grow.”
